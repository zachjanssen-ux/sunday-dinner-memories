Welcome!

First off — thank you. You're one of the first people to try Sunday Dinner Memories, and your feedback is going to help shape this app for families everywhere.

Getting Started

Sunday Dinner Memories works right in your phone's web browser — no app store download needed. Here's how to open it:

1

Open the link on your phone. Use whatever browser you normally use (Safari on iPhone, Chrome on Android).

2

Add it to your home screen so it feels like a real app. Here's how:

📱 Screenshot: "Add to Home Screen" button in your browser's share menu

On iPhone (Safari)

1

Tap the Share button at the bottom of Safari (the square with an arrow pointing up).

2

Scroll down and tap "Add to Home Screen".

3

Tap "Add" in the top right. Done! You'll see the app icon on your home screen.

On Android (Chrome)

1

Tap the three dots menu in the top right of Chrome.

2

Tap "Add to Home screen" or "Install app".

3

Tap "Add". The app will appear on your home screen like any other app.

🏠 Screenshot: The Sunday Dinner Memories app icon on a phone's home screen
Tip

Adding it to your home screen gives you the best experience — it opens full-screen without the browser toolbar.

Scanning Your First Recipe Card

This is the big one. Grab a recipe card — handwritten, typed, printed, whatever you've got — and let's turn it digital.

1

From the dashboard, tap the "Add Recipe" button (the orange button with a + sign).

2

Choose "Scan Recipe Card."

📷 Screenshot: Import options showing "Scan Recipe Card" highlighted
3

Point your camera at the recipe card. Try to get the whole card in the frame. Tap the capture button when it looks good.

4

Wait a moment. The AI is reading the handwriting and figuring out what's an ingredient, what's a step, and what the recipe is called. This usually takes 5-15 seconds.

✨ Screenshot: The loading screen that says "Reading your recipe..."
5

Review the results. You'll see the recipe title, ingredients, and instructions all filled in. Look it over — does everything look right?

6

Edit anything that needs fixing. The AI is smart, but handwriting is hard. If it misread "1 tsp" as "1 tap," just tap the field and fix it. No big deal.

7

Tap "Save Recipe." That's it! Your recipe card is now preserved forever in your family's digital kitchen.

🎉 Screenshot: A completed recipe card in the app after scanning

Tips for the Best Scan

Lighting

Good lighting makes a huge difference. Natural light near a window is best. Avoid shadows falling across the card.

Surface

Lay the card flat on a table or counter. Don't hold it in the air — it's harder to keep steady.

Hold Steady

Take a second to hold your phone still before tapping the capture button. A blurry photo makes the AI's job harder.

Handwriting

Clear handwriting works best, but the AI can handle most handwriting styles. If some words are hard to read even for a person, the AI might struggle too — that's okay, you can edit it.

What If the Scan Isn't Perfect?

No worries. Here's what to do:

Fix & Edit

After scanning, every field is editable. Tap any ingredient, instruction, or the recipe title to fix it. The original photo is always saved so you can reference it.

If a scan went really sideways, you can try again with a new photo — just go back and scan it again. Different lighting or angle sometimes helps.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this app free?

Yes! During the beta period, everything is completely free. We're building this with love and we want you to try it with no strings attached.

Will my recipes be saved if the app updates?

Absolutely. Your recipes are stored safely in the cloud. They'll be there whenever you come back, even if we update the app.

Can other people see my family's recipes?

Only people you invite to your family can see your recipes. Your family kitchen is private to your family.

What if I scan a recipe and it's totally wrong?

Delete it and try again! Sometimes a different angle or better lighting does the trick. And tell us about it — that feedback helps us improve the scanning.

Can I scan recipes that are typed or printed, not handwritten?

Yes! Typed and printed recipe cards usually scan even better than handwritten ones. Try it with whatever you have.

Do I need an internet connection?

Yes, for now. The scanning feature needs the internet to process the photo. You can browse recipes you've already saved even with a weak connection.

My camera isn't working in the app. What do I do?

Make sure you've given the app permission to use your camera. On iPhone, go to Settings > Safari > Camera and set it to "Allow." On Android, the app will ask when you first try to scan.

Can I add recipes from a website or cookbook?

Right now, you can scan physical cards, upload photos, or type recipes manually. We're working on more import options for the future!
